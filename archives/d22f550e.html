量子力学笔记（三）

发表于2022-01-14|更新于2022-01-14|量子力学
|字数总计:890|阅读时长:5分钟|阅读量:

Hilbert Space

  • The state of a system is represented by its wave function, observables are represented by operators

  • Wave functions live in Hilbert space

    • the set of all square-integrable functions

      • $\int_a^b|f(x)|^2dx$
      • $\int|\Psi|^2dx=1$

    • inner product of two functions

      • $\lang f|g\rang \equiv \int_a^b f(x)^*g(x)dx$

Observables

  • $\lang Q\rang=\lang\Psi|\hat{Q}\Psi\rang$

  • Hermitian Operator

    • $\left \langle f|\hat{Q}g\right \rangle=\left \langle \hat{Q}f|g\right \rangle $for all f(x) and all g(x)

      the outcome of measurement has got be real

    • Observables are represented by hermitian operator

      • $\hat{Q}=\hat{Q}^\dagger$

  • Determinate States

    • Determinate states of $Q$ are eigenfunctions of $\hat{Q}$
      • $\hat{Q}\Psi=q\Psi$

Eigenfunctions Of A Hermitian Operator

  • Discrete Spectra

    • Theorem1: Their eigenvalues are real

    • Theorem2: Eigenfunctions belonging to distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal

      • for degenerate state, we can use the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization proedure to construct orthogonal eigenfunctions within each degenerate space

    • Axiom: The eigenfunctions of an observable operator are complete

  • Continuous Spectra

    • 基矢间的关系、态函数向基矢投影得到系数
  • Discrete Spectra Continuous Spectra
    $<f f>=\int_a^b
    $<f_m f_n>=\delta_{mn}$
    $f(x)=\sum_{n=1}^{\infin}c_nf_n(x)$ $f(x)=\int_{-\infin}^{\infin}c§f_p(x)dp$
    $c_n=<f_n f>$

Generalized Statistical Interpretation

  • If you measure an observable $Q(x,p)$ on a particle in the state $\Psi(x,t)$, you are certain to get one of the eigenvalues of the hermitian operator $\hat{Q}(x,-i\hbar d/dx)$
    • If the spectrum of $\hat{Q}$ is discrete, the probability of getting the particular eigenvalues $q_n$ associated with the (orthonormalized) eigenfunctions $f_n(x)$ is
      • $|c_n|^2 $ where $c_n=\lang f_n|\Psi\rang$
    • If the spectrum of $\hat{Q}$ is continuous, with real eigenvalues $q(z)$ and associated (Dirac-orthonormalized) eigenfunctions $f_z(x)$, the probability of getting a result in the range $dz$ is
      • $|c(z)|^2dz $ where $c(z)=\lang f_z|\Psi\rang$
      • $\Psi(y,t)=\lang g_y|\Psi\rang$
      • $\Phi(p,t)=\lang f_p|\Psi\rang$
      • 能否根据归一化得出$\int|c(z)|^2dz=1$

The Uncertainty Principle

  • $\sigma_A^2\sigma_B^2\ge(\frac{1}{2i}\langle [\hat A, \hat B]\rangle)^2$

    • the commutator of two hermitian operators is itself anti-hermitian$(\hat{Q}^\dagger=-\hat{Q})$, and its expectation value is imaginary

  • incompatible observables

    • observables whose operators do not commute

    • do not have shared eigenfunctions

  • The Minimum-Uncertainty Wave Packet

    • $g(x)=iaf(x)$, where $a$ is real

      • 不等号成立的条件

    • $(\hat{A}-\langle A\rangle)\Psi=ia(\hat{B}-\langle B\rangle)$

    • Gaussian wave packet

  • The Energy-Time Uncertainty Principle

    • energy-time uncertainty principle
      • $\Delta t\Delta E\ge\frac{\hbar}{2}$
      • $\Delta t=\frac{\sigma_Q}{|d\lang Q\rang/dt|}$
    • generalized Ehrenfest theorem
      • $\frac{d}{dt}\lang Q\rang=\frac{i}{\hbar}\lang [\hat{iH},\hat{Q}]\rang+\lang \frac{\partial \hat{Q}}{\partial t}\rang$
      • $\sigma_H\sigma_Q\ge\frac{\hbar}{2}|\frac{d\lang Q\rang}{dt}|$

Vectors and Operators

  • Bases in Hilbert Space
    • the wave function is the component in the expansion of $|\mathcal{S}(t)\rang$ in the basis of corresponding eigenfunctions
      • $\Psi(x,t)=\lang x|\mathcal{S}(t)\rang$
      • $\Phi(p,t)=\lang p|\mathcal{S}(t)\rang$
      • $c_n(t)=\lang n|\mathcal{S}(t)\rang$
    • linear combination
      • $|\mathcal{S}(t)\rang=\int\Psi(y,t)\delta(x-y)dy=\int\Phi(p,t)\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\hbar}}e^{ipx/\hbar}dp=\sum c_ne^{-iE_nt/\hbar\psi_n(x)}$
      • in position space, $\hat{x}-x$, $|x\rang-\delta(x-y)$, $component-\Psi(x,t)$, $\mathcal{S}(t)-\Psi(x,t)$
      • $|\mathcal{S}(t)\rang$大概就是一个抽象的态，在不同空间表示不同
    • operators (representing observables) are linear transformations on Hilbert space
      • $\lang e_m|\hat{Q}|e_n\rang\equiv Q_{mn}$
      • $b_m=\sum_nQ_{mn}a_n$
    • operators look different when expressed in different bases
      • $\hat{x}=\left{\begin{matrix} x \ i\hbar\partial/\partial p \end{matrix}\right.$
      • $\hat{p}=\left{\begin{matrix} -i\hbar\partial/\partial x \ p \end{matrix}\right.$
    • actually, some systems admit only a finite number $N$ of linearly independent states
  • Dirac Notation
    • bra and ket
      • $\lang f|=\int f^*[…]dx$
      • $\lang\alpha|\to(a_1^a_2^…a_n^*)$
      • dual space
    • operator
      • projection operator
        • $\hat{P}\equiv|\alpha\rang\lang\alpha|$
        • idempotent: $\hat{P}^2=\hat{P}$
      • identity operator (completeness)
        • $\sum_n|e_n\rang\lang e_n|=1$
        • $\int|e_z\rang\lang e_z|dz=1$
      • spectral decomposition
        • $\hat{Q}=\sum_nq_n|e_n\rang\lang e_n|$
      • functions of operators
        • $f(\hat{Q})=\sum_nf(q_n)|e_n\rang\lang e_n|$
        • $e^{\hat{Q}}\equiv1+\hat{Q}+\frac{1}{2}\hat{Q}^2+\frac{1}{3!}\hat{Q}^3+…$
        • $\frac{1}{1-\hat{Q}}\equiv1+\hat{Q}+\hat{Q}^2+\hat{Q}^3+\hat{Q}^4+…$
        • $ln(1+\hat{Q})\equiv\hat{Q}-\frac{1}{2}\hat{Q}^2+\frac{1}{3}\hat{Q}^3-\frac{1}{4}\hat{Q}^4+…$
      • Changing Bases In Dirac Notation
        • 利用恒等算符方便换基底
